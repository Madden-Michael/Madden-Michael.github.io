### Overview
During my Summer 2025 internship with AFRL at Space Dynamics Lab (Albuquerque), I performed systems engineering and early mission design for a cubesat docking concept. This work was critical in defining the mission architecture and verifying feasibility before preliminary design.

### Field Work & Kickoff

![Visit to VAB](/assets/projects/afrl-sdl/AFRLVAB.JPEG)

Visiting the Vehicle Assembly Building (VAB).

### Contributions
- **Requirements**: Drafted/updated ICD-level requirements and analyzed parent-child traceability.
- **CONOPS**: Developed mission threads including nominal and off‑nominal flows with success criteria.
- **Trade Studies**: Evaluated docking architectures, sensing options, and risk drivers.
- **V&V Planning**: Mapped early verification methods to all defined requirements.

### Impact
Delivered comprehensive mission thread diagrams, requirement flowdowns, and trade study results. My focus was on ensuring clear traceability (Goals → Requirements → Verification) to reduce technical risk early in the life cycle.
